BLR

Broad-Line Region Geometry & Dynamics

Gas region near the black hole that emits broad emission lines, located at only ~tens - hundreds of light-days away from the central black hole, responsible for broad emission lines with their high doppler velocity. Since they are photo-ionized by the radiation from the central black hole, they emit delayed radiation which reflects the variability of it

  • Continuum–line lag measurements
  • Velocity-delay maps and dynamical modeling.
  • Mass scale calibration and uncertainties.

AGN Cosmology

Black Holes Extended Towards the Cosmos

Black holes are small in size compared to galaxies, clusters, and the universe. Yet, AGNs are one of the most luminous objects in the universe, enabling us to use them as standard candles for cosmology. Furthermore, since host galaxies co-evolve with their central black holes, exploring distant (i.e. past) AGNs provides us crucial information about the evolution of galaxies in the early universe.

  • Understanding the co-evolution of AGN and galaxy.
  • Mapping the galaxy growth history.
  • Calibration of the size-luminosity relation for AGN as standard candle.
