Broad Line Region

Gas region near the black hole that emits broad emission lines, located at only ~tens - hundreds of light-days away from the central black hole, responsible for broad emission lines with their high doppler velocity. Since they are photo-ionized by the radiation from the central black hole, they emit delayed radiation which reflects the variability of it

  • Continuum–line lag measurements
  • Velocity-delay maps and dynamical modeling.
  • Mass scale calibration and uncertainties.

GALAXY MORPHOLOGY

Cosmology

Black holes are small in size compared to galaxies, clusters, and the universe. Yet, AGNs are one of the most luminous objects in the universe, enabling us to use them as standard candles for cosmology. Furthermore, since host galaxies co-evolve with their central black holes, exploring distant (i.e. past) AGNs provides us crucial information about the evolution of galaxies in the early universe.

  • Line diagnostics and photoionization modeling.
  • Spatially resolved IFU mapping of winds.
  • Momentum/energy-loading vs. luminosity.

Dust Torus

Fundamental structure in AGNs, surrounding the central BH and accretion disk with dense gas and dust. It absorbs radiation from the accretion disk and re-emits it in the IR, shaping observed AGN properties and obscuring our view along certain sightlines.

  • Time domain analysis by modeling the variability of the accretion disk together with the delayed IR response of the dust.
  • Torus geometry and dust sublimation radii.

M–σ relation

Quantifies the correlation between SMBH mass and bulge stellar velocity dispersion to probe black hole–galaxy coevolution and to estimate BH masses.

  • Template fits and cross-correlation pipelines.
  • Systematics from broad vs. narrow lines.
  • Quality flags and uncertainty modeling.
